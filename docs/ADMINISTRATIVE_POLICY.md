**Level 2 - Employee Areas**:
- Break rooms, general office space
- Access: All employees with ID badge

**Level 3 - Restricted Areas**:
- Warehouse, inventory storage, server room
- Access: Authorized personnel only
- Sign-in/sign-out log required

**Level 4 - Highly Restricted**:

## 3. Clean Desk Policy

### 3.1 Desk Security

**End of Day Requirements**:
- âœ… Lock all confidential documents in drawer/cabinet
- âœ… Shred unnecessary confidential papers
- âœ… Log out of computer (or shut down)
- âœ… Lock workstation (Windows+L)
- âœ… Secure cash/valuables in safe
- âœ… Close and lock windows (ground floor)

**During Work Day**:
- âœ… Lock screen when leaving desk (>2 minutes)
- âœ… Do not leave confidential documents unattended
- âœ… Position monitor away from public view
- âœ… Use privacy screen for sensitive work

### 3.2 Document Handling

**Confidential Documents**:
- Print only when necessary
- Collect prints immediately
- Do not leave in printer tray
- Shred when no longer needed
- Store in locked cabinet

**Disposal**:
- **Confidential**: Cross-cut shred (P-4 level)
- **Internal**: Regular shred or recycle
- **Public**: Recycle

**Shredding Locations**:
- Shredder in each office area
- Locked shred bins for bulk shredding
- Weekly shredding service for large volumes

### 3.3 Workstation Security

**Computer Security**:
- Password-protected screen saver (5 minutes)
- Automatic lock after 10 minutes inactivity
- Full disk encryption enabled
- Antivirus updated daily
- Firewall enabled

**Physical Security**:
- Cable lock for laptops
- Monitor positioned away from windows/doors
- No sticky notes with passwords
- Secure USB ports (disabled by default)

### 5.2 Confidential Email Handling

**Sending Confidential Data**:
- âœ… Use encrypted email (S/MIME or password-protected PDF)
- âœ… Verify recipient address
- âœ… Use BCC for mass emails (privacy)
- âœ… Include confidentiality footer

**Confidentiality Footer**:
```

```

### 5.3 Phishing & Security

**Red Flags**:
- Urgent requests for passwords/credentials
- Unexpected attachments
- Suspicious sender addresses
- Requests to click links
- Grammar/spelling errors
- Requests for money transfers

**If Suspicious**:
1. Do NOT click links or open attachments
2. Do NOT reply
3. Forward to security@siifmart.et
4. Delete original email

**Phishing Simulation**:
- Quarterly phishing tests
- Training for employees who click
- No punishment for reporting

### 5.4 Communication Channels

**Approved Channels**:
- Email (business communication)
- Microsoft Teams / Slack (internal chat) - if implemented
- Phone (urgent matters)
- In-person (confidential discussions)

**Prohibited Channels** (for business):
- Personal WhatsApp/Telegram
- Personal social media DMs
- Personal email accounts
- Public forums

## 6. Third-Party Access Policy

### 6.1 Vendor Access Levels

**Level 1 - No System Access**:
- Suppliers, delivery drivers
- Physical access only
- Supervised at all times

**Level 2 - Limited System Access**:
- Maintenance contractors
- Temporary staff
- Read-only access
- Time-limited (contract duration)

**Level 3 - Full System Access**:
- IT consultants, auditors
- Approved by Super Admin
- NDA required
- Supervised sessions
- All actions logged

### 6.2 Vendor Onboarding

**Requirements**:
1. Background check (for recurring vendors)
2. NDA signed
3. Security training completed
4. Access request approved
5. Temporary credentials issued
6. Access reviewed monthly

**Offboarding**:
1. Access revoked immediately upon contract end
2. Return all company property
3. Confirm data deletion
4. Exit interview

### 6.3 Data Sharing with Vendors

**Permitted**:
- Minimum data necessary for service
- Aggregated/anonymized data preferred
- Encrypted transmission

**Prohibited**:
- Customer payment card data
- Employee salary data
- Trade secrets
- Unencrypted data transmission

**Vendor Requirements**:
- Must have own privacy policy
- Must comply with data protection laws
- Must notify of breaches within 24 hours
- Must delete data upon contract termination

## 7. Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery

### 7.1 Backup Policy

**Backup Schedule**:
- **Daily**: Incremental backups (11:00 PM)
- **Weekly**: Full backups (Sunday 2:00 AM)
- **Monthly**: Archive backups (1st of month)

**Backup Locations**:
- **Primary**: On-site NAS (Network Attached Storage)
- **Secondary**: Cloud storage (encrypted)
- **Tertiary**: Off-site physical backup (monthly)

**Backup Retention**:
- Daily backups: 7 days
- Weekly backups: 4 weeks
- Monthly backups: 12 months
- Annual backups: 7 years (financial data)

**Backup Testing**:
- Monthly restore test (random file)
- Quarterly full restore test
- Annual disaster recovery drill

### 7.2 Disaster Recovery Plan

**Recovery Time Objectives (RTO)**:
- **Critical Systems** (POS, WMS): 4 hours
- **Important Systems** (Email, HR): 24 hours
- **Standard Systems** (Reports): 72 hours

**Recovery Point Objectives (RPO)**:
- **Critical Data**: 1 hour (max data loss)
- **Important Data**: 24 hours
- **Standard Data**: 7 days

**Disaster Scenarios**:
1. **Power Outage**: UPS (2 hours) + Generator (48 hours)
2. **Hardware Failure**: Hot spare servers, 4-hour replacement
3. **Cyber Attack**: Isolate, restore from clean backup
4. **Fire/Flood**: Activate off-site backup, relocate to backup site
5. **Data Breach**: Execute incident response plan

**Emergency Contacts**:
- IT Support: [24/7 Number]
- Super Admin: [24/7 Number]
- Backup Site: [Address + Contact]
- Cloud Provider Support: [Number]

### 7.3 Incident Response Team

**Team Members**:
- **Incident Commander**: Super Admin
- **Technical Lead**: IT Support
- **Communications Lead**: HR Manager
- **Business Lead**: Finance Manager

**Activation**:
- Any critical incident triggers team activation
- Team assembles within 2 hours
- Incident command center established
- Stakeholders notified

## 8. Vendor Management Policy

### 8.1 Vendor Categories

**Category A - Critical Vendors**:
- Cloud hosting provider
- Payment processor
- ERP/WMS software vendor
- **Due Diligence**: Extensive, annual audit

**Category B - Important Vendors**:
- Internet service provider
- Hardware suppliers
- Security services
- **Due Diligence**: Standard, biannual review

**Category C - Standard Vendors**:
- Office supplies
- Cleaning services
- Utilities
- **Due Diligence**: Basic, annual review

### 8.2 Vendor Selection Criteria

**Evaluation Factors**:
1. **Security**: Data protection practices, certifications
2. **Reliability**: Uptime SLA, track record
3. **Compliance**: Regulatory compliance, audits
4. **Financial**: Stability, insurance coverage
5. **Support**: Response time, availability
6. **Cost**: Total cost of ownership

**Required Certifications** (for Category A):
- ISO 27001 (Information Security)
- SOC 2 Type II (Service Organization Controls)
- PCI-DSS (if handling payments)

### 8.3 Vendor Contracts

**Required Clauses**:
- Data protection and privacy
- Confidentiality (NDA)
- Security requirements
- Breach notification (24 hours)
- Right to audit
- Termination conditions
- Data deletion upon termination
- Liability and indemnification

**Contract Review**:
- Legal review for all Category A vendors
- Annual contract renewal review
- Performance review quarterly

### 8.4 Vendor Monitoring

**Ongoing Monitoring**:
- Monthly performance reports
- Quarterly security assessments
- Annual audits (Category A)
- Incident tracking

**Performance Metrics**:
- Uptime/availability
- Response time
- Issue resolution time
- Security incidents
- Compliance violations

**Vendor Termination**:
- 30-day notice (standard)
- Immediate termination for security breach
- Data return/deletion within 7 days
- Access revocation immediate
- Final audit within 30 days
